  • Wayfaring Tweets

    As people's thoughts seem to be discarded in 140 characters or less, wayfaring-tweets initially sought to recapture these arrays of memories in the context of roadtrips. Developed in the final week of my General Assembly WDI experience, my final project implements Backbone.js, the Twitter Rest API, and includes a gorgeous Leaflet map.

  • Wildfire

    Wildfire is an events-promotion tool, designed to help planners generate 'buzz' surrounding their occasion by encouraging potential attendees to log in to Twitter and debate about its possible location and agenda. As tweets are collected, event followers are given additional clues and an increasingly detailed map to help fuel the fire. Deployed with JavaScript + jQuery + Ajax, various Ruby gems, Google Maps, and the Twitter API, this rails project is the product of a week-long General Assembly team sprint emphasizing group scrumming and collaboration, agile development, and version control (git, github).

    see it live

  • SupaTravel

    Supatravel is a travel themed photo-blogging platform. As the project was intended to simulate an industry scenario, the rails app was developed over a three day sprint in which General Assembly students collaborated while our instructor played the part of a needy and indecisive client. The project provided us with our first major introductory crash course to git and github, and allowed us to experiment with the 'gmaps4rails' gem and 'paperclip'.

    see it live

  • PaintMyCity

    Paintmycity is a platform for users to geo-tag urban artwork in Washington DC. My first Rails application, developed in a weelong sprint at General Assembly, aims to add a guerilla, decentralized, city-wide art gallery to our nation's capital. The project allowed me to experiment with rolling my own user authentication, while experimenting with the gmaps4rails gem.
